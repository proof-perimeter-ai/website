NDA review automation is the application of contract AI to the legal department's highest-volume request: the non-disclosure agreement — arriving daily from sales, procurement, partnerships, and HR, materially similar across instances, and yet each requiring review because the deviations that matter (a perpetual term, a one-way obligation disguised as mutual, a residuals clause, an IP assignment smuggled into confidentiality language) hide in individually negotiated text. NDAs became contract automation's beachhead precisely because of this profile: high volume, standard structure, well-understood risk points, and legal teams tired of spending scarce hours on them.

The automated review runs the playbook systematically: classification confirms the document is what it claims (and which flavor — mutual, one-way, deal-specific); clause extraction locates the operative provisions (definition of confidential information, term and survival, permitted disclosures, return/destruction, remedies, governing law); each is scored against the organization's positions — acceptable, negotiate, escalate — with deviations explained and, in the redlining tier, counter-language proposed in the document's own defined terms. The output fits the volume reality: clean NDAs approved at intake speed (many organizations route them entirely self-service — the salesperson getting same-hour turnaround with legal never touching the file), deviations returned with specific asks, and only the genuine outliers reaching a lawyer, pre-analyzed.

The pattern's significance exceeds NDAs themselves: it is the template for playbook-driven contract automation generally — prove the loop (extract, compare, propose, escalate) on the contract type where stakes are modest and volume is motivating, instrument the outcomes (turnaround time, touchless rate, escalation precision, downstream dispute incidence), then extend the machinery to DPAs, vendor terms, and order forms as confidence and playbooks mature. And the standing disciplines apply even at NDA stakes: citations per finding, versioned playbooks (the positions are policy — governed like policy), and sampling review that keeps the touchless tier honest.
