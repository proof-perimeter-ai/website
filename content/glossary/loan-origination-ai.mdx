Loan origination AI is the automation of lending's document machinery: the application pack — identity documents, income evidence (payslips, tax returns, employer letters), bank statements, existing-debt records, and for secured lending the collateral file (valuations, titles, insurance) — ingested, classified, extracted, verified, and assembled into a decision-ready case. Origination is where lending's economics and experience are decided: document friction drives abandonment (applicants walk when the checklist churns for weeks), manual processing drives cost per loan, and verification quality drives the credit and fraud losses that surface years later.

The pipeline composes this glossary's financial entries into one flow. Bundle splitting and classification sort the uploaded pack; per-type extraction structures each document; cross-document verification does the underwriting-relevant reading — declared income against payslip figures against statement deposits, employer names consistent, addresses agreeing across proofs, the applicant's documents actually the applicant's (entity resolution plus ID verification). Fraud screening runs the forensics: tampered statements, template payslips, synthetic identity signals. Completeness logic tracks the file against the product's requirements and chases gaps at upload time; and the assembled case — facts, confidences, flags, provenance — feeds decisioning, whether automated within policy bands or packaged for the underwriter with the anomalies pre-surfaced.

The regulatory frame shapes the automation's boundaries: adverse decisions carry explanation and human-involvement requirements in most jurisdictions (fair-lending scrutiny extending to the models' disparate impact), document retention and audit obligations attach to the file, and the extraction provenance — which page established the income figure the decision used — is the record examiners and dispute processes consult. The measured wins are the industry's standard story: days-to-decision compressed toward minutes for clean files, cost per origination falling, and underwriter attention migrating from document assembly to the judgment lending actually pays them for.
