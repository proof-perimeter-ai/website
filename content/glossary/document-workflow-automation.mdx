Document workflow automation is the orchestration of a document's entire operational journey — capture, classification, extraction, validation, review, approval, delivery, archival — as a managed process rather than a series of human handoffs. The workflow engine holds the map (what steps, in what order, under what conditions) and the state (where each document is, what it awaits); the document AI components supply the reading and judgment at each step; and the humans participate at defined stations — review, approval, exception resolution — with work delivered to them rather than found by them.

The design questions are process-engineering questions. Sequencing and parallelism: which steps can run concurrently (extraction on all pages at once) and which gate others (validation before approval). Conditionality: the branches that content triggers — the high-value invoice detouring through second approval, the incomplete pack looping into a chase-and-wait state that resumes on arrival. Time semantics: SLAs per step, escalations when clocks run, and the long-lived waits (days for a customer's missing document) that require durable state, not a process holding a thread. Exception paths designed as first-class flows rather than dead ends. And observability throughout: every document's position visible, every transition logged, aggregate flow metrics feeding the operational dashboards that tell you where the process actually bottlenecks.

The relationship to neighboring terms is compositional: business process automation is the broader discipline; document routing is one function within the workflow; agentic workflows replace the static map with runtime planning; workflow agents staff the stations with AI. What document workflow automation itself delivers is the reliable spine — the guarantee that every document entering the process exits it through a defined path, with its state always known and its history always reconstructable, at whatever mix of machine and human effort the organization's measured confidence currently justifies.
