Legal due diligence AI is the application of document intelligence to the legal workstream of transactions: reviewing the target's contracts, corporate records, litigation files, permits, IP registrations, and employment documents for the findings a buyer's counsel must surface — change-of-control provisions requiring consent, assignment restrictions, unusual liabilities and indemnities, non-competes that travel, litigation exposure, defects in corporate authorization. The data room is legal diligence's arena, its volumes are unreviewable exhaustively by humans on deal timelines, and its findings move price, structure, and conditions.

The AI workflow mirrors — and completes — the associate playbook. Document inventory and classification first: what the data room contains, organized against the diligence checklist, with the gaps flagged (the material contract referenced but not produced — itself a finding). Clause-level review across the contract population: the change-of-control screen run on *every* agreement, not the sampled top fifty; the termination, exclusivity, MFN, and liability provisions extracted, classified against market norms, and ranked by deviation. Cross-document assembly: the guarantee web, the intercompany arrangements, the license chains that only cohere when read together. Output lands as structured findings — issue, severity, affected documents, quoted language, citation — feeding the diligence report that counsel curates rather than compiles.

The measured effects match the pattern across legal AI: coverage rising from samples to populations, review timelines compressing from weeks toward days, and junior hours migrating from reading to verifying and judging. The disciplines are the field's constants — verifiable citations, coverage transparency, privilege and confidentiality handling worthy of deal secrecy (data-room contents being among the most sensitive documents in commerce, with in-controlled-infrastructure processing frequently a condition of engagement) — plus diligence's own: the AI surfaces and evidences; materiality, deal judgment, and the negotiation the findings arm remain the lawyers'.
